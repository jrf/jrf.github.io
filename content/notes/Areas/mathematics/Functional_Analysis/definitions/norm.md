## Definition

A **norm** on a vector space $V$ is a function $\|\cdot\|: V \to \mathbb{R}$ satisfying:

1. **Positive definiteness**: $\|x\| \geq 0$, with equality iff $x = 0$
2. **Absolute homogeneity**: $\|\alpha x\| = |\alpha| \|x\|$ for all scalars $\alpha$
3. **Triangle inequality**: $\|x + y\| \leq \|x\| + \|y\|$

## From Inner Products

Given an [inner product](/notes/areas/mathematics/functional_analysis/definitions/inner_product/), the induced norm is:



$$
\|x\| = \sqrt{\langle x, x \rangle}
$$



For functions:



$$
\|f\| = \sqrt{\int |f(x)|^2 \, dx}
$$



## Intuition

The norm measures the "size" or "magnitude" of a vector or function:
- In physics, $\|f\|^2$ often represents **energy**
- The norm generalizes the concept of length from Euclidean space

## Common Norms

| Norm | Definition | Name |
|------|------------|------|
| $\|x\|_1 = \sum |x_i|$ | Sum of absolute values | $L^1$ / Manhattan |
| $\|x\|_2 = \sqrt{\sum |x_i|^2}$ | Square root of sum of squares | $L^2$ / Euclidean |
| $\|x\|_\infty = \max |x_i|$ | Maximum absolute value | $L^\infty$ / Supremum |
