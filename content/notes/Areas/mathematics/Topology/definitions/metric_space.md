## Definition

A **metric space** is an ordered pair $(X, d)$ where $X$ is a set and $d: X \times X \to \mathbb{R}$ is a [metric](/notes/areas/mathematics/topology/definitions/metric/) on $X$.

## Examples

- $(\mathbb{R}^n, d)$ with the Euclidean [metric](/notes/areas/mathematics/topology/definitions/metric/) $d(x,y) = \sqrt{\sum (x_i - y_i)^2}$
- Any [normed](/notes/areas/mathematics/functional_analysis/definitions/norm/) [vector space](/notes/areas/mathematics/linear_algebra/definitions/vector_space/) $(V, \|\cdot\|)$ with $d(x,y) = \|x - y\|$
- The discrete metric space: $d(x,y) = \begin{cases} 0 & x = y \\ 1 & x \neq y \end{cases}$

## Key Concepts

- A [subset](/notes/areas/mathematics/set_theory/definitions/subset/) $U \subseteq X$ is **open** if for every $x \in U$, there exists $\varepsilon > 0$ such that $B(x, \varepsilon) \subseteq U$
- The **open ball** of radius $\varepsilon$ centered at $x$ is $B(x, \varepsilon) = \{y \in X : d(x, y) < \varepsilon\}$
- Every metric space is a [topological space](/notes/areas/mathematics/topology/definitions/topological_space/) via the [metric](/notes/areas/mathematics/topology/definitions/metric/) topology
