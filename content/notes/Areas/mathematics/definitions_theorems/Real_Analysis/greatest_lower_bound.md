## Definition

A real number $i$ is the *greatest lower bound* (or *[infimum](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/real_analysis/infimum/)*) for a set $A \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ if:

1. $i$ is a [lower_bound](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/real_analysis/lower_bound/) for $A$: $i \leq a$ for all $a \in A$
2. $i$ is the greatest such bound: if $b$ is any [lower_bound](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/real_analysis/lower_bound/) for $A$, then $b \leq i$

Notation: $i = \inf A$ or $i = \text{glb}(A)$.

See also: [infimum](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/real_analysis/infimum/), [supremum](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/real_analysis/supremum/).
