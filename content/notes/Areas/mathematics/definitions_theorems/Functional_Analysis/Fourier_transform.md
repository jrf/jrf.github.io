## Definition

The *Fourier transform* is an integral transform that decomposes a [function](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/set_theory/function/) into its constituent frequencies. For a function $f \in L^1(\mathbb{R}^n)$, the Fourier transform $\hat{f}$ (or $\mathcal{F}\{f\}$) is defined by:



$$
\hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} f(x) e^{-2\pi i \xi \cdot x} \, dx
$$



The *inverse Fourier transform* recovers the original function:



$$
f(x) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} \hat{f}(\xi) e^{2\pi i \xi \cdot x} \, d\xi
$$



## Properties

1. **Linearity**: $\mathcal{F}\{af + bg\} = a\mathcal{F}\{f\} + b\mathcal{F}\{g\}$

2. **Convolution Theorem**: $\mathcal{F}\{f * g\} = \mathcal{F}\{f\} \cdot \mathcal{F}\{g\}$

3. **Shift Theorem**: $\mathcal{F}\{f(x - x_0)\} = e^{-2\pi i \xi \cdot x_0} \hat{f}(\xi)$

4. **Scaling Theorem**: $\mathcal{F}\{f(ax)\} = \frac{1}{|a|}\hat{f}\left(\frac{\xi}{a}\right)$

5. **Parseval's Theorem**: $\int |f(x)|^2 \, dx = \int |\hat{f}(\xi)|^2 \, d\xi$

## Remarks

- The Fourier transform is an [automorphism](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/abstract_algebra/automorphism/) on the [Schwartz_space](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/functional_analysis/schwartz_space/) $\mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R}^n)$.
- The transform extends to [tempered_distribution](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/functional_analysis/tempered_distribution/)s via duality.
- The [convolution](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/real_analysis/convolution/) in one domain corresponds to multiplication in the other domain.
