# Span

## Definition

The set of all [linear_combination](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/linear_algebra/linear_combination/)s of a [list](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/linear_algebra/list/) of vectors $v_{1},\ldots,v_{m}$ in $V$ is called the *span* of $v_{1},\ldots,v_{m}$ denoted $\text{span }\left. \left( v_{1},\ldots,v_{m} \right) \right.$. In other words, pick



$$
\text{ span }\left. \left( v_{1},\ldots,v_{m} \right) \right. = \{ a_{1}v_{1} + \cdots + a_{m}v_{m}:a_{1},\ldots,a_{m} \in \mathbb{F}\}
$$



The *span* of the empty [list](/notes/areas/mathematics/definitions_theorems/linear_algebra/list/) $\left. () \right.$ is defined to be $\{ 0\}$.

If $\text{span }\left. \left( v_{1},\ldots,v_{m} \right) \right.$ equals $V$, we say that $v_{1},\ldots,v_{m}$ *spans* $V$.
