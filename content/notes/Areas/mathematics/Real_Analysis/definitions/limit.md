A **limit** describes the value that a [function](/notes/areas/mathematics/set_theory/definitions/function/) or [sequence](/notes/areas/mathematics/real_analysis/definitions/sequence/) approaches as the input or index approaches some value.

## Limit of a Sequence

A [sequence](/notes/areas/mathematics/real_analysis/definitions/sequence/) $(a_n)$ has limit $L$ if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $N \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $n \geq N$ implies $|a_n - L| < \epsilon$.



$$
\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = L
$$



See [convergence](/notes/areas/mathematics/real_analysis/definitions/convergence/) for more details.

## Limit of a Function

A [function](/notes/areas/mathematics/set_theory/definitions/function/) $f$ has limit $L$ as $x$ approaches $c$ if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta > 0$ such that $0 < |x - c| < \delta$ implies $|f(x) - L| < \epsilon$.



$$
\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = L
$$



See [functional_limit](/notes/areas/mathematics/real_analysis/definitions/functional_limit/) for more details.

## Properties

Limits satisfy the [Algebraic_Limit_Theorem](/notes/areas/mathematics/real_analysis/definitions/algebraic_limit_theorem/):
- $\lim(a_n + b_n) = \lim a_n + \lim b_n$
- $\lim(c \cdot a_n) = c \cdot \lim a_n$
- $\lim(a_n \cdot b_n) = \lim a_n \cdot \lim b_n$
